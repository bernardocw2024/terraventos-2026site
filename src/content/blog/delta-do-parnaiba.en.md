Talk about the Ceará coast and everyone thinks of Jericoacoara. But there is a stretch at the **far west of the state** that almost no one knows — and it may be the most special of all: **Bitupitá**, on the border with Piauí, right at the **gateway to the Parnaíba Delta**.

## Not the end of the map. The start of the delta.

Bitupitá closes the Ceará coastline to the west and sits inside the **Parnaíba Delta Environmental Protection Area**. It belongs to the **Rota das Emoções**, the route linking Jericoacoara, the Delta and the Lençóis Maranhenses.

In practice, that means Bitupitá **isn't an isolated spot in the middle of nowhere** — it's the entrance to a recognised, still under-explored ecosystem.

## What lies around it

- **Ceará's largest mangrove** and the state's largest fishing community.
- **Five rivers meeting the sea** — Timonha, Ubatuba, Chapada, Carapinas and Camelo.
- **Over six kilometres of shifting dunes**, likened to a pocket Lençóis.
- The **Praia do Arrombado lagoon**, a natural pool of crystal-clear water between fixed dunes.
- The **Bitupitá lighthouse**, a lookout over beaches, dunes and forest.

> One of Brazil's last preserved virgin stretches of coast, with community-based tourism.

## Why it matters for investors

Three forces usually precede a coast's appreciation: **access, tourism and infrastructure**. The Rota das Emoções already delivers an established route; the wind (June to December) sustains kitesurfing; and the delta gives Bitupitá a natural edge few destinations have.

Buying early, on a still-frontier stretch, is exactly what set apart those who entered Jeri twenty years ago from those who arrived later.
